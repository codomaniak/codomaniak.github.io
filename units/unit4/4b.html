Grammar: prepositions of time in, at, on

on + days, dates

at + time of day

Listen and read the interview with Christina East. Answer the questions.

  • When is Nothing Day?
  • Why Nothing Day?
  • What do people do on Nothing Day?
  • What is Nothing Day?

Christina East is a British mental health specialist She thinks it's time for a new special holiday, called Nothing Day.

The idea comes from Harold Coffin, an American journalist. It's a,day for nothing. No parties, no gifts, no cards. It's a time to have a break, to sit and do nothing.

It's on January 16th. The first Nothing Day was in 1973.

There are special days for everything. In March we have Mother's Day and in June we have Father's Day. In October there's United Nations Day and Halloween and in April there's Earth Day.

Ideally people do nothing. But that's very difficult. Here are some suggestions:

In the morning, wake up when you like. Have a relaxing breakfast. Do nothing.

In the afternoon, go for a walk. Sit in a park. Do nothing.

At night, telephone an old friend and talk. Read a book, or go to bed. Do nothing.
