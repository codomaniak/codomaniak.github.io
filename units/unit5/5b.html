Grammar: past simple was/were

The past tense of the verb be is was/were.

Our holiday was lovely.

We were in Canada.

The negative is wasn't/weren't.

I wasn't very happy.

The shops weren't open.

Read the sentences. Then make answers with the words in brackets.

We were in Dublin. (Glasgow) - No, we weren't. We were in Glasgow.

The hotel was expensive, (cheap)

It was in a noisy part of town, (quiet)

It was sunny and warm, (rainy and cold)

Our tour guide was an Irish man. (Scottish man)

We were there for a week, (ten days)
