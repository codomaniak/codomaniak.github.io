Today we will

  • give personal information
  • count from 11 to 100
  • learn to form plurals

Grammar

Plurals

  • a doll + s = two dolls
  • bus - buses, class — classes, toothbrush - toothbrushes, match – matches, fox - foxes
  • a toy - two toys
  • a lady - two ladies
  • fe - lives, scarf – scarves

Exceptions

  • a child — children
  • a foot - feet
  • a tooth - teeth
  • a woman - women
  • a man - men
  • a mouse - mice

A or an?

  • apple
  • box
  • eraser
  • chair
  • orange
  • hat
  • lemon
  • lamp
  • shape
  • house
  • fox
  • name

he, she, it, we, you or they?

  • a girl
  • his mother
  • mothers
  • his cat
  • I and Anton
  • You and Alla
  • My mother and I
  • You and I

Listen to and read the dialogue

Admin worker: International Student ID card?

Student: Yes, that’s right.

AW: Have you got the form?

S: Yes, here it is.

AW: OK, let’s see. Name – Ahmed, surname – Saeed, age 14, address ... (pause) ... oh, what’s your address?

S: 14 Spring Avenue …

AW: 14 Spring Avenue ...

S: Leicester.

AW: Can you spell that, please?

S: L-E-I-C-E-S-T-E-R.

AW: What’s your postcode?

S: LE 14 2GZ.

AW: LE 14 2GS.

S: No, 2GZ.

AW: OK, 2GZ. And what’s your nationality?

S: I’m British.

AW: What’s your school?

S: Newtown Secondary School. N-E-W-T-O-W-N.

AW: And what’s your date of birth?

S: The 2nd of June 1998.

AW: 02/06/1998 (the second of the sixth, 1998). Have you got a photo?

S: Yes, here you are.

AW: Thank you. One moment, please ... (pause) ... and here’s your card.

S: Thanks. Bye.
