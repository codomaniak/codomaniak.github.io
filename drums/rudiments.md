# 🥁 Development Plan: Rudimental Vocabulary
## From Novice → Advanced (6-Month Program)

---

### 🎯 The Goal
**By the end of 6 months, you will:**
- Know and play all 26 standard American rudiments with proper technique.
- Apply rudiments to the drum set (orchestration around toms, cymbals, and snare).
- Play Swiss rudiments and hybrid rudiments with control and speed.
- Use rudiments as **melodic phrases** in fills, grooves, and solos — not just exercises.

---

## 📅 Month 1: The Foundation — 5 Core Rudiments
**Focus:** Build proper grip, stroke technique, and evenness. Learn the 5 essential rudiments that form the basis of all others.

| Week | Rudiments | Exercises | Daily Practice (15–20 min) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Week 1** | **Single Stroke Roll** | • Play 8th notes → 16th notes → 32nd notes. <br>• Accent the first note of every group of 4. | • 5 min: Single strokes at 60 BPM (8ths). <br>• 5 min: Increase to 80 BPM. <br>• 5 min: Accented singles (accent on 1). |
| **Week 2** | **Double Stroke Roll** | • Play "RRLL RRLL" with even volume. <br>• Start slow — focus on 2nd stroke being as strong as the 1st. | • 5 min: Double strokes at 60 BPM. <br>• 5 min: Increase to 80 BPM. <br>• 5 min: Open roll (RRLL RRLL) at 100 BPM. |
| **Week 3** | **Paradiddle** | • Play RLRR LRLL. <br>• Accent the first note of each group. <br>• Move accent to different notes. | • 5 min: Paradiddle slow (60 BPM). <br>• 5 min: Accented paradiddle (accent on R). <br>• 5 min: Accent on L. |
| **Week 4** | **Flam & Drag** | • **Flam:** Grace note + primary stroke (both hands). <br>• **Drag:** Two grace notes + primary stroke. | • 5 min: Flams (alternating hands). <br>• 5 min: Drags (alternating hands). <br>• 5 min: Combine Flams + Drags in a 4-bar pattern. |

**🎯 Milestone:** You can play all 5 rudiments cleanly at 80–100 BPM with even dynamics and proper stick control.

---

## 📅 Month 2: Expand to 13 Essential Rudiments
**Focus:** Add more rudiments, focus on stick height control, and start rudimental "phrasing" (playing them in musical groups).

| Week | New Rudiments | Exercises | Daily Practice (20–25 min) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Week 1** | **Single Paradiddle-Diddle** <br>(RLRRLL or LRLLRR) | • Add an extra double stroke to the paradiddle. <br>• Play as 6-note pattern. | • 5 min: Paradiddle-diddle slow (60 BPM). <br>• 10 min: Increase speed gradually (70–90 BPM). <br>• 5 min: Move accents to different notes. |
| **Week 2** | **Double Paradiddle** <br>(RLRLRR or LRLRLL) | • Two single strokes + one double stroke pattern. <br>• Play with even volume and flow. | • 5 min: Double paradiddle slow. <br>• 10 min: Speed up to 80 BPM. <br>• 5 min: Add accents on the first note of each group. |
| **Week 3** | **Flam Accent & Flam Tap** | • **Flam Accent:** Flam + two single strokes. <br>• **Flam Tap:** Flam + two taps (one on each hand). | • 5 min: Flam accent (alternating hands). <br>• 5 min: Flam tap. <br>• 10 min: Combine both into a 4-bar phrase. |
| **Week 4** | **Drag Paradiddle & Single Ratamacue** | • **Drag Paradiddle:** Drag + paradiddle. <br>• **Single Ratamacue:** Drag + 4 notes. | • 5 min: Drag paradiddle (slow). <br>• 10 min: Single ratamacue. <br>• 5 min: Play both in sequence. |

**🎯 Milestone:** You can play 13 rudiments (including the first 5) cleanly at 80–100 BPM with controlled stick heights and dynamic accents.

---

## 📅 Month 3: All 26 Rudiments — Part 1 (Rolls & Diddles)
**Focus:** Complete the 26 American rudiments. This month covers roll-based and diddle-based rudiments.

| Week | New Rudiments | Exercises | Daily Practice (20–25 min) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Week 1** | **5-Stroke Roll, 7-Stroke Roll, 9-Stroke Roll** | • Play rolls with alternating hands. <br>• Accent the first and last stroke. | • 5 min: 5-stroke roll (RRLLR or LLRRL). <br>• 5 min: 7-stroke roll. <br>• 10 min: 9-stroke roll. <br>• 5 min: Play all in sequence. |
| **Week 2** | **10-Stroke Roll, 11-Stroke Roll, 13-Stroke Roll** | • Extend rolls — focus on evenness and speed. <br>• Start slow and build. | • 5 min: 10-stroke roll (RRLLRRLLRR). <br>• 5 min: 11-stroke roll. <br>• 10 min: 13-stroke roll. <br>• 5 min: Sequence all rolls. |
| **Week 3** | **15-Stroke Roll & 17-Stroke Roll** | • Long rolls — maintain even volume throughout. <br>• Practice with a metronome. | • 5 min: 15-stroke roll. <br>• 5 min: 17-stroke roll. <br>• 10 min: Alternate between rolls. <br>• 5 min: Play full 26-rudiment sequence (rolls only). |
| **Week 4** | **Review all roll-based rudiments** | • Play all rolls in order. <br>• Record yourself and evaluate evenness. | • 10 min: Warm-up with singles + doubles. <br>• 15 min: Play all rolls slowly (60–80 BPM). <br>• 5 min: Increase speed gradually. |

**🎯 Milestone:** You can play all roll-based rudiments with consistent volume, proper alternating sticking, and controlled acceleration.

---

## 📅 Month 4: All 26 Rudiments — Part 2 (Flam & Drag Families)
**Focus:** Complete the remaining 26 rudiments — flam-based and drag-based patterns.

| Week | New Rudiments | Exercises | Daily Practice (20–25 min) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Week 1** | **Flam Paradiddle, Flam Paradiddle-Diddle** | • Combine flams with diddle patterns. <br>• Focus on grace note + primary stroke clarity. | • 5 min: Flam paradiddle. <br>• 10 min: Flam paradiddle-diddle. <br>• 5 min: Alternate between both. |
| Week 2 | **Flam Drag, Flamacue** | • **Flam Drag:** Flam + drag. <br>• **Flamacue:** Flam + 4-note phrase. | • 5 min: Flam drag. <br>• 10 min: Flamacue. <br>• 10 min: Play both in sequence with accents. |
| Week 3 | **Drag Paradiddle #1, Drag Paradiddle #2** | • Different sticking variations of drag paradiddle. <br>• Focus on grace note speed. | • 5 min: Drag paradiddle #1. <br>• 10 min: Drag paradiddle #2. <br>• 10 min: Alternate between both. |
| Week 4 | **Single, Double, Triple Ratamacue** | • **Single:** Drag + 4 notes. <br>• **Double:** Drag + 6 notes. <br>• **Triple:** Drag + 8 notes. | • 5 min: Single ratamacue. <br>• 5 min: Double ratamacue. <br>• 10 min: Triple ratamacue. <br>• 5 min: Play all three in sequence. |

**🎯 Milestone:** You have now learned all 26 standard American rudiments. You can play them cleanly at 80–100 BPM with proper dynamics and sticking.

---

## 📅 Month 5: Applying Rudiments to the Drum Set
**Focus:** Move from the practice pad to the drum set — orchestrate rudiments around toms, cymbals, and snare drum.

| Week | Focus | Exercises | Daily Practice (20–25 min) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Week 1** | **Rudiments around the kit** | • Move single/double strokes around toms. <br>• Play paradiddles on snare → hi-tom → floor tom → ride cymbal. | • 5 min: Single strokes around the kit. <br>• 10 min: Paradiddles moving from snare to toms. <br>• 5 min: Double stroke rolls on different surfaces. |
| **Week 2** | **Rudiments as fills** | • Create 4-bar fills using only rudiments. <br>• Example: 2 bars groove → 2 bars rudimental fill. | • 5 min: Play groove + rudiment fill (single/double rolls). <br>• 10 min: Groove + paradiddle fill. <br>• 5 min: Groove + flam/drag fill. |
| **Week 3** | **Rudiments with feet** | • Play rudiments with hands while feet keep time. <br>• Start with basic rock beat underneath. | • 5 min: Feet-only groove. <br>• 10 min: Add hand rudiments (singles, doubles, paradiddles). <br>• 5 min: Add flams and drags. |
| **Week 4** | **Rudimental "Phrasing"** | • Play rudiments in musical phrases. <br>• Example: 2 bars of paradiddle + 2 bars of roll + 2 bars of flam. | • 5 min: Warm-up with singles. <br>• 15 min: Create 8-bar phrases using 3–4 rudiments. <br>• 5 min: Record and evaluate musicality. |

**🎯 Milestone:** You can move rudiments fluidly around the drum kit and use them as fills or phrases within a groove context.

---

## 📅 Month 6: Advanced — Swiss Rudiments & Hybrids
**Focus:** Expand beyond the 26 — learn Swiss rudiments and modern hybrid rudiments. Apply them melodically and improvisationally.

| Week | New Rudiments | Exercises | Daily Practice (25–30 min) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Week 1** | **Swiss Army Triplet** <br>(RLR LRL or RLL RLL) | • A combination of single + flam. <br>• Focus on even triplet feel. | • 5 min: Swiss triplet slow (60 BPM). <br>• 10 min: Increase to 80 BPM. <br>• 10 min: Move around the kit. |
| **Week 2** | **Swiss Paradiddle & Swiss Flam** | • **Swiss Paradiddle:** Swiss triplet + paradiddle. <br>• **Swiss Flam:** Flam + Swiss triplet. | • 5 min: Swiss paradiddle. <br>• 10 min: Swiss flam. <br>• 10 min: Combine both into a 4-bar phrase. |
| **Week 3** | **Hybrid Rudiments** <br>(e.g., Herta, Book Report, Cheese, Pataflafla) | • Learn modern hybrid rudiments. <br>• Focus on high-speed control and accents. | • 5 min: Herta (RRLL RLL). <br>• 5 min: Book Report (RLLR LLRR). <br>• 10 min: Cheese (flam + tap). <br>• 5 min: Pataflafla (flam + drag). |
| **Week 4** | **Melodic Rudimental Soloing** | • Improvise a 1–2 minute solo using only rudiments. <br>• Mix standard, Swiss, and hybrid rudiments. | • 10 min: Compose a structured solo. <br>• 15 min: Improvise freely with rudiments. <br>• 5 min: Record, listen, and refine. |

**🎯 Milestone:** You can play Swiss and hybrid rudiments with speed and control, and use them melodically in improvisation.

## 🧰 Additional Tips for Rudimental Development

| Tip | Details |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Always use a metronome** | Start at 40–60 BPM for new rudiments. Increase by 5 BPM only when clean. |
| **Practice both hands equally** | Start every exercise with both right and left hands. |
| **Focus on stick heights** | Low strokes = soft, high strokes = loud. Control height for dynamics. |
| **Record slow-motion video** | Check your grip, wrist motion, and finger control. |
| **Play on different surfaces** | Practice pad → snare → toms → cymbals. Each surface changes rebound. |
| **Use "chunking"** | Break long rudiments into smaller groups (e.g., 4-note chunks). |
| **Apply rudiments to grooves** | A rudiment is only useful if you can play it in a musical context. |
| **Join a drum corps or band** | Playing with others forces you to play rudiments in time and in context. |
